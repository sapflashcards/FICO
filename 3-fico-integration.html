üéØ Why This Matters in Real Life

For Your Company:

  • Better Decisions: Know exactly which departments are spending what
  • Accurate Pricing: Calculate true product costs including overhead
  • Budget Control: Track if departments stay within budget
  • Compliance: Meet legal reporting requirements while managing operations

Example Impact: If your Marketing department goes over budget, CO catches it immediately. FI just shows "marketing expense" - CO shows "Digital Marketing Team spent $5000 over budget on ads."

üìö Key Takeaways

1. One Transaction, Two Views
Every business transaction gets recorded once but viewed differently by FI and CO
2. Account Type = Traffic Controller
The account type decides if data flows to FI, CO, or both
3. Not Everything Goes to CO
CO only cares about operational costs/revenues - not assets, loans, or non-business items
4. Cost Centers = Who Spent It
When CO gets involved, it always asks "which department/project spent this?"

üéì Remember This Simple Rule:

FI = What the government/investors see
CO = What managers use to run the business
